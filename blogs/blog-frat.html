Being In A Fraterntiy

September 2023

Introduction

I am very fortunate to be in my current situation; I go to The Cooper Union, a highly respected institution that houses, educates, and develops New York Cities’ finest engineers while also being a brother of NYU’s AEPI Alpha Chapter. Yes, I am part of an NYU fraternity and yes, it’s completely legal. AEPI is considered an “off-campus” fraternity and we can recruit non-nyu students.

I have a massive appreciation for Greek Life, and I believe it can positively impact almost anyone. It's not just great for the outgoing extroverts who want to party in college; it's also great for those quiet introverts who get pulled in too.

Coming into college I knew I wasn’t going to be a party person, but during my freshman year I knew I needed at least some type of social life outside of school. Cooper Union has a very dry social atmosphere and coincidentally, through a friend, I found out about the fraternity. At the very last minute and on a whim, I went to one of AEPI’s rush events in the spring of my freshman year and I loved it. I loved the atmosphere, the people, the work ethic, and the cumulative wealth of our alumni. (Yes, Mark Zuckerburg has a big portion of that).

Not everyone in the fraternity is great, but there are more than a handful of people that I look up to and use as motivation to do better in my life and I couldn’t ask for more than that - a community of like minded individuals who want to conquer the world.

Social & Rush Chair

I tend to take extreme ownership over the things I do and I have a very obsessive personality. So naturally, I ran for and got my hands on some positions in the fraternity. I am currently the social chair and used to be a rush chair the semester before. Some of the things I do now are, finding venues, planning events, inviting people, graphic design of flyers, delegating tasks, repairing speakers mid party, risk mitigation, decorations, aesthetics, automation, finances, and many more.

Some interesting takeaways (and things I’ve learned) from being social and rush chair:

Theme is king.

Choosing the right theme is the most important part of being a social chair. You can put in hours of work reaching out to potential attendees, you can spend hours researching and finding the best venue and decorations, but if you don’t make the invite appealing/unique and you don’t make the theme easy to dress for, people just won’t show up. From experience, the best parties have been themed Euphoria, Outer Banks, Whiteout, and Barbie. All of which were popular shows or movies at the time that women can easily dress for.

Numbers Game.

It’s all about the numbers. Whether it’s inviting girls to a party or potential rushes, quantity is always better than quality.
