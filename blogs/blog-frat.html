Being In A Fraterntiy

September 2023

Introduction

I am very fortunate to be in my current situation; I go to The Cooper Union, a highly respected institution that houses, educates, and develops New York Cities’ finest engineers while also being a brother of NYU’s AEPI Alpha Chapter. Yes, I am part of an NYU fraternity and yes, it’s completely legal. AEPI is considered an “off-campus” fraternity and we can recruit non-nyu students.

I have a massive appreciation for Greek Life, and I believe it can positively impact almost anyone. It's not just great for the outgoing extroverts who want to party in college; it's also great for those quieter introverts who get pulled in too.

Coming into college I knew I wasn’t going to be a party person, but during my freshman year I knew I needed at least some type of social life outside of school. Cooper Union has a very dry social atmosphere and coincidentally, through a friend, I found out about the fraternity.

At the very last minute and on a whim, I went to one of AEPI’s rush events in the spring of my freshman year and I loved it. I loved the atmosphere, the people, the work ethic, and the cumulative wealth of our alumni. (Yes, Mark Zuckerburg has a big portion of that).

Not everyone in the fraternity is great, but there are more than a handful of people that I look up to and use as motivation to do better in my life and I couldn’t ask for more than that - a community of like minded individuals who want to conquer the world.
