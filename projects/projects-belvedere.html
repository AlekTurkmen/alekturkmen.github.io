Project Belvedere

September 2024 - November 2024

Overview / TDLR

During college, I became pretty good at securing desirable internships. This led me to consider creating a business to teach other college students my networking skills.

Inspired by Alex Hormozi’s principles on Lead Generation and Offer Creation, I applied his strategies in a real-world context. The plan was simple: create an offer, and if people showed interest, build it out; if not, only minimal time would be lost.

In March 2024, I gathered a Cooper alumni database of ~6,600 profiles, I then segmented alumni into career categories (e.g., Bioengineering, Finance) and utilized cold emailing to reach ~900 students, achieving a 50% open rate and 0.85% conversion rate. By September 2024, had validated the concept with initial users, refining aspects like pricing and marketing. To appeal broadly, I printed and distributed psychology-based flyers. My business model charged a $100 fee only upon successful job placement, with a referral option to waive the fee.

March 2024

Background

I kicked off a project to build a complete alumni database of my college’s alumni. The alumni portal has many access restrictions, so I whipped up an automated scraping solution. I dove into reverse engineering API calls using the browser's inspect tool and tweaked the URLs to pull in more data. I also had to get around some anti-bot measures by mimicking a regular web browser's behavior, which meant sending verification cookies and using user-agent headers. In the end, I managed to scrape all the info and organize it into a CSV file, sorting the alumni by career and industry from 1941 to 2028 - creating networking resource gold.

I also data-mined the info by scraping LinkedIn for names and sorting alumni into different careers and industries. Categories include things like finance, engineering, biotech, startups, research, etc.
